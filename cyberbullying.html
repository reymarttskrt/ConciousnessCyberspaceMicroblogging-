Cyber Bullying
Cyber Bullying is a common cybercrime that mostly happens, especially among teenagers. Moreover, it works by using electrical means. Cyber Bullying is the use of cell phones, instant messaging, e-mail, chat rooms or social networking sites such as Facebook and Twitter to harass, maliciously embarrass, threaten or intimidate someone.

How to avoid Cyber Bullying

  • Don't post online that should be keep in private
  • Avoid putting inappropriate photos online because they can be the fuel that cyberbullies use.
  • Manage privacy settings online
  • Limit access to your devices and accounts
  • Think before you post

Who are the ones that you can call?

  • If you experience cyberbullying inside the school, report it to your teacher or principal. But if you experience this outside of school, keep your evidence or proof and report it to law enforcement so they can take action to prevent and keep others from experiencing cyberbullying.

How to determine if you are experiencing Phishing?

  • If someone humiliating you by posting/sharing embarrassing videos or images
  • If someone tagging you inappropriately in an image
  • Spreading rumors about you or lies online
  • If you receive a threats from someone
  • Imitating others online
