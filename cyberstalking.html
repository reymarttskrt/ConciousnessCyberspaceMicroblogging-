Cyber Stalking
Cyberstalking is a crime committed when someone uses the internet and other technologies to harass or stalk another person online. Furthermore, it can include defamation, false accusations, teasing and even extreme threats.

How to avoid Cyber Stalking

  • Be careful what personal information you share online.
  • Create a different or dump email for registering in social media sites.
  • Enable strict privacy settings on social media platforms.
  • Don't provide sensitive or personally identifiable information such as your full name, phone number, home address or workplace to individuals you meet online, in chat rooms, social media or gaming platforms.

Who are the ones that you can call?
  • Do not hesitate to call 117. Report the cyber-harassment or cyberstalking to the police district where your business and home are located, along with all documentation. If you have proof of the perpetrator's identity, file a restraining order because you never know when the harassment will stop.
How to determine if you are experiencing Cyber Stalking?

  • Someone anonymously sending you too many unwanted or inappropriate messages.
  • Someone anonymously liked your old post on social media.
  • Someone manipulates you into talking to them online. Be wary.
  • They know everything about you, even your private life.
